Product Overview

Most online transactions and even mobile banking require two-step authentication and the OTP (One Time Password) sent through SMS is one of those two verification steps.

Most online transactions and even mobile banking require two-step authentication and the OTP (One Time Password) sent through SMS is one of those two verification steps. Often a times users need OTP when they attempt to sign in to their accounts from different devices such as computers, smartphones, or tablets. One Time Password, also known as One Time Pin, are not as vulnerable as regular or static passwords. Since OTPs cannot be reused even by the person who receives it for signing up to its account, they are highly secure.

What is an OTP (One Time Password)?
OTP is a string of numbers or characters that verifies a user for a single transaction or login attempt. In simple words, a One-Time Password is a password that is valid for one login session only and cannot be reused after a specific time duration. It provides an extra layer of security for user data whenever it attempts to sign-in to its account or use ATM. They are crucial to prevent theft attempts or online fraud practices. OTPs are usually received through an SMS. Many companies, including financial institutions and telecom service providers, use Short Message Service (SMS) for providing a temporary pin for a second verification or authentication factor via text.

Benefits of One Time Passwords (OTP)

Resistance Against Replay Attacks: OTPs are not susceptible to replay attacks. Unlike static passwords, where an unauthorized user intercepts data transmission, tracks it, and uses it to gain access to account or system information. However, thatâ€™s not a case with OTPs as once the user enters the OTP to access its account, the code becomes invalid for reuse.

Reduced Risks of Invalid Access

Users who are not aware of security best practices tend to use the same account credentials for different accounts. In case these credentials are leaked, all the accounts are vulnerable to fraud and information loss. One Time Passwords offers the utmost security against data breach and unauthorized access to data.

Randomly Generated Codes

OTPs are extremely difficult to guess as they are generated using random algorithms. These codes act as an additional data layer due to their short-term validity and incapability to reuse the code. Since the OTPs are generated using unique algorithms, they are hard to use by unauthorized users and prevent invalid account access.

Easy & Simple Adoption

One Time Passwords are relatively easy for companies to integrate into their user authentication strategies. Since the cryptic nature of the OTPs makes it hard for people to memorize these codes, it's considerably easy for the security teams to distribute these tokens over SMS for easy access.

OTP codes are the right way to minimize cybercrime risks such as hacking attempts, identity thefts, phishing, etc. These codes are valid only for a couple of minutes; there's no point of reuse once it's used or gets expired.
